        STEP 1: CHOOSING THE RIGHT CUT 
    

    


    

    Preferred Cuts: For pan-frying, thicker cuts such as ribeye, striploin (New York strip), or even a well-trimmed sirloin work exceptionally well. These cuts offer a balance between a rich fat content and a robust beefy flavor.
    

 Thickness: Aim for a steak that is at least 1 to 1.5 inches thick. A thicker cut helps develop a nice sear and crust while ensuring the interior remains tender and juicy during high-heat cooking.
    

 Fat Integration: The marbling should be well-integrated into the meat rather than clumped in one area, which indicates a higher quality cut and optimal flavor release when cooked.
    













    

    

        STEP 2:  PREPARATION OF THE MEAT  
    

    


    

    Pre Marinate Prep: Use paper towels to pat the steak dry This helps the marinade adhere better.
    Score (Optional): Lightly score the surface if you have a thicker cut. This can help the marinade penetrate deeper.
    

 Marinating Process: Mix all the marinade ingredients in a large bowl. Place the steak in a resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the steak, ensuring itâ€™s well-coated.
    Seal the bag or cover the dish. Place it in the refrigerator. 
    

 Marinating Time:  For most steaks, 30 minutes to 2 hours is sufficient. Tougher or thicker cuts can benefit from longer marination (up to overnight).
    Note: If your marinade is heavy on acidic components, avoid marinating too long (beyond 24 hours) to prevent the meat from becoming mushy.
    





      










    






    


    

        STEP 3: COOKING THE STEAK 
    

    

    

    

    

    

    

    


    

    Initial Sear: As A5 Wagyu is a very fatty cut, aim to utilise all the flavour of the fat, start the pan with some olive oil and sear the fatty side of the steak to get out the flavour of the fat
    

 Cooking: Cook the steak on low heat for around 3-4 minutes. Note: You do not need to flip the steak during this phase of the cooking. 
    

 Flavour Bathing: Put the butter, beef bouillon and honey into the pan and use a spoon to gently baste the sauce over the steak to create a sweet umami flavour. Make sure to flip the steak and baste both sides.
    

 Resting : A good tasting steak always need to rest before being cut open, take the steak off the pan and rest it for 1 minute prior to consuming.
